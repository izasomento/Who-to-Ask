# Letters for Minerva

A structured decision-support tool designed to help prospective university applicants—particularly those with limited access to formal college counseling—evaluate and select their best recommendation letter writers.

## The Problem

For many students, especially first-generation and international applicants, the hidden curriculum of university admissions is a significant barrier. Choosing a recommender is often treated as a simple checklist item, but it is actually a high-stakes strategic choice. Without guidance, students may choose people who know them well but cannot speak to the specific dimensions (like independence or curiosity) that schools like Minerva value most.

## 🛠️ The Approach

This tool functions as a structured reasoning aid. It prompts students to analyze their relationships through eight key dimensions of academic and personal growth, turning intuition into actionable data.

- **Human-Centered:** Prioritizes student agency and reflection over automated answers.
- **Transparent:** Shows the exact logic behind every score to help students understand *why* certain factors matter.
- **Privacy-First:** No data collection, no login, and no backend. All logic runs entirely in the browser.

## 📊 How the Scoring Works

The model evaluates potential recommenders across four core pillars:

1.  **Depth of Connection:** How well does this person actually know your character and work?
2.  **Context of Interaction:** Can they speak to your performance in multiple or high-impact environments?
3.  **Recent Growth:** Is their perspective current, reflecting who you are today?
4.  **Advocacy Potential:** Are they likely to be an enthusiastic and specific advocate for your global path?

The tool provides a **Scoring Breakdown** for every evaluation, showing exactly how each factor contributed to the final result (e.g., +3 for deep connection, -2 for urgent deadlines).

## ⚖️ Assumptions & Limitations

- **Honest Reflection:** The tool's effectiveness depends on the user's honest assessment of their relationships.
- **Qualitative over Quantitative:** While it uses numbers, the goal is to spark qualitative reasoning, not to provide a correct mathematical answer.
- **Guidance, not Guarantee:** This is a reflection aid. It does not predict admissions outcomes or guarantee the quality of a resulting letter.

## 🌍 Who This Helps

- **First-Generation Students:** Those navigating the process without family experience to lean on.
- **International Applicants:** Students from systems where recommendation letters may follow different cultural norms.
- **Self-Directed Learners:** Any student looking for a structured way to manage their application strategy under uncertainty.

## 💡 Why This Matters

Educational access isn't just about financial aid; it's about **information equity**. By open-sourcing the reasoning behind strong recommendations, we help level the playing field for students who are navigating the complex world of global higher education on their own.
