How the Evaluation Works

Letters for Minerva uses a structured reasoning model to help you identify which recommenders can provide the strongest evidence for your application.

The Four Pillars

Our model evaluates every potential recommender across four core dimensions that admissions officers at high-rigor institutions value most:

  • Depth of Connection: How well does this person actually know your character and work? High scores require more than just a good grade; they require personal insight.
  • Context of Interaction: Perspectives from multiple environments (e.g., a teacher who is also your club advisor) often lead to more holistic letters.
  • Recent Growth: Is their perspective current? A teacher from three years ago may not be able to speak to the person you are today.
  • Advocacy Potential: Are they likely to be an enthusiastic advocate? An unenthusiastic letter can often be more damaging than no letter at all.

Assumptions & Limits

While this tool provides structured data, it makes several key assumptions:

  • Honest Assessment: The tool relies on your honest reporting of the relationship.
  • Specificity Matters: The model assumes that specific anecdotes are always better than general praise.
  • No Outcome Guarantee: This is a reflection aid. It does not guarantee admission or the final quality of the written letter.
